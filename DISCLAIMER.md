# DISCLAIMER - Governance materials; no professional advice

This repository contains governance materials for **SOLOMON DAO LLC**
(the "DAO"), including draft and proposed DAO proposals, memoranda,
templates, and related documents (collectively, "Repository Materials").

## 1. What this repository is (and is not)

- **Governance coordination and recordkeeping.** Repository Materials are
published to coordinate and record governance activity and to provide a
durable, auditable history (including commit history).
- **Not professional advice.** Repository Materials are not, and must not
be construed as, **legal advice**, **tax advice**, **financial advice**,
or any other professional advice to any person.

## 2. Proposals can become binding on the DAO (that still is NOT "legal advice")

Some Repository Materials may be submitted to the DAO's governance process
and, if approved/ratified by the DAO's voting or resolution mechanisms, may:
- become an adopted DAO Resolution and/or part of the DAO's Company
  Agreement;
- be executed on-chain (e.g., via an execution bundle); and/or
- be filed or delivered to third parties (including an RMI registered
  agent or other service providers) where required or desirable.

**If and only if a proposal is adopted through the DAO's governance
mechanisms, the resulting governance outcome may be legally binding on the
DAO and persons exercising authority under the Company Agreement** to the
extent provided by the Company Agreement and applicable law.

That governance effect does **not** turn these materials into professional
advice to participants or the public. It is the DAO's internal
decision-making and record of that decision.

## 3. No attorney-client relationship; no duty of care

- No Repository Material, discussion, pull request, issue, comment, or
  communication creates an **attorney-client**, **fiduciary**, or other
  special relationship between:
  - any author/contributor and any reader; or
  - the DAO (or any person exercising authority under the
    Company Agreement) and any reader.
- The DAO and contributors do not undertake any duty to provide advice,
  to monitor compliance for others, or to update/maintain Repository
  Materials for any reader's benefit.

## 4. Open submissions; drafts; "presence ≠ endorsement"

- **Anyone may draft or submit** materials (subject to repository
  controls). Drafts may be incomplete, wrong, or inconsistent with later
  adopted resolutions.
- The **presence** of a proposal or document in this repository does
  **not** mean it is adopted, correct, endorsed, or reviewed by counsel.
- Unless explicitly stated in an adopted DAO Resolution (or otherwise in
  an authoritative DAO act), no contributor is acting as counsel to the
  DAO or any other person.

## 5. Canonical record; incorporation by reference; conflicts

- **Canonical governance record.** The canonical record of what the DAO
  adopted is the DAO's governance system / on-chain record, the SOP
  Registry, and any formally executed or filed instruments (as applicable).
- **Incorporation by reference is allowed.** A DAO Resolution may
  incorporate by reference a specific repository path and/or **commit hash**
  (and, on adoption, an annotated git tag may be used to memorialize the
  canonical commit) (or content identifier) to precisely identify the
  adopted text. A DAO Resolution may also reference an annotated git
  **tag** (e.g. a `@passed` or `@executed` tag) as a human-friendly alias
  for a specific commit. This is a mechanism for precision and auditability,
  not an invitation to treat authors as providing advice.
- **If there is a conflict** between a repository copy and an
  adopted/authoritative record, the adopted/authoritative record controls.

## 7. Practical guidance (what to do if you care about legal accuracy)

- Treat drafts as drafts.
- Verify whether a proposal was adopted (and the adopted text) via the
  DAO's canonical governance record and any relevant filed/executed
  instruments.
- Get independent counsel for your situation.
