# IP License Framework of SOLOMON DAO LLC

**Status:** Pre-formation (executed by Organizer; not adopted by on-chain vote)

**Effectiveness:** This Framework takes effect on the Effective Date of the Company's Certificate of Formation.

**PRIMARY INSTRUMENT.** This IP License Framework is a binding primary instrument of the Company Agreement and governs the licensing of Protocol IP.

---

This IP License Framework is intended to comply with the Decentralized Autonomous Organizations Act of 2022 (as amended) (the "DAO Act"), the Limited Liability Company Act, 1996 (as amended) (to the extent not superseded by the DAO Act), and the Decentralized Autonomous Organization Regulations 2024 (the "DAO Regulations").

## Article I -- Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

This Framework establishes the terms on which the Company may license Protocol IP to third parties and receive contributions to Protocol IP.

### 1.2 Intellectual Property Firewall

1. **"Protocol"** in this Framework has the same meaning as **"Protocol IP"** as defined in Operating Agreement §2.1(17) - design, documentation, code, repositories, and other intellectual property. It does not mean an operating business, service, issuer, custodian, counterparty, or other person or entity.

2. Protocol IP is licensed under terms that require licensees to comply with applicable law and assume responsibility for their own deployments and operations. The Company does not operate, control, administer, or maintain any deployment of Protocol IP and shall not be deemed to do so. Licensee conduct is independent and is not attributable to the Company.

3. Adoption of this Framework, any license, or any Resolution does not obligate the Company to operate, provide, maintain, or support any deployment or service. No such obligation should be inferred from treasury directions or governance documentation.

### 1.3 Scope

This Framework:

1. establishes how the Company grants licenses to Protocol IP;
2. establishes the terms applicable to all licenses;
3. establishes what licensees must and must not do;
4. establishes how the Company receives contributions to Protocol IP; and
5. establishes what the Company does not do.

This Framework is part of the Company Agreement and must be interpreted as a whole with the other instruments of the Company Agreement.

### 1.4 IP-Only; Separate Agreements

This Framework governs the licensing of Protocol IP and the receipt of contributions to Protocol IP only. Any master agreement, statement of work, services arrangement, commercial contract, or other agreement involving a Licensee (including any arrangement with additional parties) is separate and does not modify, supplement, or expand any License or the Company's role, except to the extent a signed writing expressly states otherwise and is adopted in the manner required by the Company Agreement and recorded in the SOP Registry.

## Article III -- Grant of License

### 3.1 License Grant

The Company may grant Licenses to Protocol IP by Resolution, or pursuant to authority delegated under the Founding Charter to the Head Steward or any Subcommittee established under it acting within its mandate, and (where required) subject to ratification in accordance with the Founding Charter. Each License shall be recorded in the IP License & Assignment Register and in the SOP Registry.

### 3.2 Terms

Each License shall specify:

1. the scope of permitted use;
2. any territorial or other restrictions;
3. the term of the License;
4. any fees payable; and
5. any other conditions.

### 3.3 Licensee Declaration Requirement

1. Each License shall include a **Licensee Declaration** section in which the Licensee sets out, in its own words and at its sole discretion, the parameters of its intended use of Protocol IP.

2. The Company does not dictate, direct, approve, or supervise the content of any Licensee Declaration. The Licensee Declaration reflects the Licensee's own independent determination of its compliance posture, risk appetite, and business strategy.

3. The Company grants broad permission to use Protocol IP for any lawful purpose. Any restriction, limitation, or exclusion stated in a Licensee Declaration is the Licensee's own self-imposed constraint. Such constraints are not imposed by the Company and do not reflect any direction or control by the Company.

4. Licensee Declarations may include, without limitation:
   - territorial scope (e.g., intended markets, excluded jurisdictions);
   - activity scope (e.g., production, development, testing);
   - regulatory posture (e.g., applicable regimes, excluded categories); and
   - use case (e.g., intended application of Protocol IP).

5. The Company makes no representation or warranty regarding the adequacy, accuracy, or legal effect of any Licensee Declaration. Each Licensee is solely responsible for determining what restrictions, if any, are appropriate for its circumstances.

### 3.4 Non-Exclusive

Unless a Resolution expressly provides otherwise, all Licenses are non-exclusive. The Company remains free to grant Licenses to other persons and to use, develop, and deal with Protocol IP.

### 3.5 Revocable

All Licenses are revocable. The Company may revoke, narrow, suspend, or reassign any License only where either (a) the Company has adopted a Resolution doing so (a "Revocation Resolution") or (b) such action is taken pursuant to authority delegated under the Founding Charter to the Head Steward or any Subcommittee established under it acting within its mandate, and (where required) is subject to ratification in accordance with the Founding Charter. Any such action shall be recorded in the SOP Registry.

The Company may revoke, narrow, suspend, or reassign a License only where it is determined in good faith that one or more of the following applies:

1. the Licensee breaches the terms of the License;
2. the Licensee breaches mandatory law;
3. continued licensing would create disproportionate risk to the Company, meaning a material and non-trivial risk of one or more of the following (as reasonably assessed in good faith):
   a. misuse, misappropriation, or unauthorized attribution of Licensed IP;
   b. misrepresentation that the Licensee or any Implementation is operated, endorsed, or controlled by the Company;
   c. sanctions, AML/CTF, fraud, or other illegality exposure reasonably attributable to the continued existence of the permission grant itself (and not to ordinary independent operations of the Licensee);
   d. a material security incident affecting the integrity, attribution, or provenance of Licensed IP (including compromised signing, registry integrity, or impersonation); or
   e. material regulatory exposure reasonably attributable to continued licensing being inconsistent with the Company's public disclosures, compliance posture, or documented governance constraints;
4. the Resolution granting the License so provides.

For avoidance of doubt, (i) the Company has no duty to investigate Licensee operations or to seek Licensee internal records in order to form any such determination; (ii) the Company may rely on the Licensee Declaration, publicly available information, and/or credible third-party notices; and (iii) revocation, narrowing, or suspension is a limitation of permission only and does not create any right of control, supervision, or management over the Licensee.

### 3.6 No Transfer

A Licensee may not assign, transfer, or sublicense any License without prior approval by Resolution.

### 3.7 Recording and Confidential Particulars

Licenses, Licensee Declarations, and licensee particulars are recorded in the SOP Registry. For record-keeping, the IP License & Assignment Register is maintained as a canonical document within the SOP Registry repository, and the Public Record for each License may reference the applicable register entry (including by identifier, path, or hash). Each License entry is split into (a) a "Public Record" and (b) "Confidential Particulars". The Public Record contains non-confidential metadata and a cryptographic hash commitment (e.g., SHA-256) to the then-current "License Package" (being the License, Licensee Declaration, and any referenced schedules). Confidential Particulars are maintained off-chain and, to the extent stored in the SOP Registry, are maintained only in hashed and/or encrypted form and must be convertible to legible written form within a reasonable time where required by applicable law.

Confidential Particulars are limited to what is necessary to evidence a valid arm's-length license grant and its economics, such as:

1. legal name of the Licensee entity;
2. jurisdiction of formation and existence/standing status;
3. effective date, scope label, and any conditions precedent;
4. fee model (including royalty schedule references) and payment address(es);
5. authorised signatory evidence for acceptance of the Licensee Declaration; and
6. a notice contact for License-related communications.

Confidential Particulars do not include, and the Company does not require or request, Licensee operational or implementation information (including customer lists, vendor contracts, bank statements, internal ledgers, deployment infrastructure, source code, trading/treasury strategy, or privileged communications), except to the extent such material is strictly required to satisfy a valid legal demand addressed to the Company.

**Member access.** Except where disclosure is required by applicable law, access to Confidential Particulars (if any are maintained in unhashed form) may be granted to Members only pursuant to a Resolution and subject to any confidentiality undertakings specified in that Resolution. Otherwise, Members may verify integrity via the Public Record hash commitments.

A License may reference an entity to be formed. The License becomes effective only when the referenced entity exists and any conditions specified in the granting Resolution are satisfied.

### 3.8 License is Not a Right

A License is permission only. A License does not confer any right, entitlement, or claim against the Company. A Licensee has no vested interest in the continuation of any License.

### 3.9 No Control Through Licensing

The grant of a License, the recording of a Licensee Declaration, or the existence of multiple Licensees with different Declarations does not constitute:

1. direction, control, or supervision of any Licensee by the Company;
2. coordination or orchestration of Licensee activities by the Company;
3. approval or endorsement of any Licensee's business, compliance posture, or Licensee Declaration; or
4. a common enterprise, joint venture, or unified operation among the Company and its Licensees.

Each Licensee operates independently. The fact that one Licensee restricts its activities to certain jurisdictions or purposes while another Licensee operates differently reflects the independent decisions of independent entities, not direction by the Company.

2. is solely responsible for its own deployments and operations;
3. is solely responsible for compliance with all laws applicable to it;
4. is solely responsible for its relationships with its own customers and counterparties;
5. is solely responsible for determining and implementing its own Licensee Declaration; and
6. may not bind, represent, or speak on behalf of the Company.

### 4.3 No Reverse Attribution

Licensee conduct, operations, deployments, products, services, customers, Licensee Declarations, and liabilities are not attributable to the Company. The Company does not operate, provide, maintain, support, or control any Licensee's business or any Implementation.

### 4.4 No Reverse Entitlement

A License does not grant the Company or Members any right to the Licensee's business, assets, revenues, customers, or intellectual property beyond the terms of the License.

### 4.5 No Agency

No Licensee is an agent of the Company. No Licensee has actual, apparent, or ostensible authority to bind the Company or to act on the Company's behalf.

The Company is not an agent of any Licensee. The Company has no authority to bind any Licensee or to act on any Licensee's behalf.

Any representation by a Licensee that it acts as agent of the Company, or any representation by any person that the Company acts as agent of a Licensee, is unauthorized and void.

### 4.6 Licensees and Members

This Framework governs commercial licensing relationships with Licensees. It does not govern the relationship between the Company and its Members, which is governed by the Operating Agreement and the Founding Charter.

A Licensee that is also a Member has the rights and obligations of a Member under the Operating Agreement and the Founding Charter, and the rights and obligations of a Licensee under this Framework. The relationship of Licensee does not confer any additional Membership rights, and the relationship of Member does not confer any additional licensing rights.

### 4.7 No Transitive Rights

A License does not confer on any Licensee any right against any Member, and does not confer on any Member any right against any Licensee.

No Licensee has any right against any other Licensee by reason of their respective Licenses.

No Licensee may assert any claim through the Company against any Member or against any other Licensee.

The Company is not an intermediary, conduit, or representative between Licensees, between Licensees and Members, or between Members and any Licensee's customers or counterparties.

### 4.8 Licensee's Independent Activities

A License relates only to Protocol IP. The Company does not restrict, supervise, or have any interest in a Licensee's activities unrelated to Protocol IP.

Each Licensee is expected to conduct its own independent business, maintain its own relationships, and operate its own infrastructure. A Licensee that has no substantial business or operations independent of Protocol IP may be treated as presenting disproportionate risk under Section 3.5.

### 4.9 No Inference from Licensee Declarations

The content of any Licensee Declaration is determined solely by the Licensee. No inference of Company direction, control, approval, or supervision may be drawn from:

1. the existence of Licensee Declarations;
2. similarities or differences between Licensee Declarations of different Licensees;
3. the specificity or generality of any Licensee Declaration;
4. the jurisdictions included or excluded in any Licensee Declaration; or
5. changes to any Licensee Declaration over time.

## Article VIII -- Contributions and Assignments

### 8.1 Contributions to Protocol IP

The Company may receive contributions to Protocol IP, including code, documentation, specifications, and other intellectual property, by assignment or license.

### 8.2 Assignment Terms

Contributions may be assigned to the Company on terms approved by Resolution and recorded in the IP License & Assignment Register.

Where assignment is not practicable, the Company may accept a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free license with rights to sublicense, modify, and incorporate the contribution into Protocol IP.

### 8.4 Contributors Are Not Licensees

A person who contributes to Protocol IP is not, by that fact alone, a Licensee. Contribution does not confer any right to use Protocol IP beyond the contribution itself.

### 8.5 Contributors Are Not Members

A person who contributes to Protocol IP is not, by that fact alone, a Member. Contribution does not confer Membership or any Membership Interest.

### 8.6 No Reverse Obligation

The Company has no obligation to accept, use, maintain, or incorporate any contribution. Acceptance of a contribution does not create any ongoing relationship or obligation.

### 8.7 Background IP

Contributors retain ownership of background intellectual property that is not specific to Protocol IP. Where background IP is incorporated into a contribution, the contributor grants the Company a license sufficient to use, modify, and sublicense Protocol IP without restriction.

### 8.8 Specific License Terms

Specific Protocol IP may contain license terms that further restrict or specify conditions of use. Such terms are Tertiary Instruments and are subject to the order of precedence established in the Company Agreement.

Further restrictions, defaults, or standard terms for licensing may be established in the SOP Registry.
