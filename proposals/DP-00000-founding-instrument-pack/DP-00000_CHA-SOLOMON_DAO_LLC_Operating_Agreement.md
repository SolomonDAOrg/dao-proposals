# Operating Agreement of SOLOMON DAO LLC

**Status:** Pre-formation (executed by Organizer; not adopted by on-chain vote)

**PRIMARY INSTRUMENT.** This Operating Agreement is a binding primary instrument of the Company Agreement and governs the Company, its Members, and the Representative Agent.

**Effectiveness.** This Operating Agreement becomes effective on the Effective Date specified in Section 1.1(2). Post-formation governance actions may ratify, amend, or implement provisions as supplementary evidence, but are not a condition to effectiveness.

---

## NOTICE OF RESTRICTIONS ON DUTIES AND TRANSFERS

The rights of members in a decentralized autonomous organization may differ materially from the rights of members in other resident domestic or non-resident domestic limited liability companies. This Chapter, underlying smart contracts, certificate of formation and limited liability company agreement, if applicable, of a decentralized autonomous organization may define, reduce or eliminate fiduciary duties and may restrict transfer of ownership interests, withdrawal or resignation from the decentralized autonomous organization, return of capital contributions and dissolution of the decentralized autonomous organization.

## Article I -- Organization

### 1.1 Formation and Status

1. **Company.** This Operating Agreement (this "Agreement") governs SOLOMON DAO LLC (the "Company" or the "DAO"), a Marshall Islands DAO LLC formed under the DAO Act, the LLC Act (to the extent not superseded), and the DAO Regulations.

2. **Effective Date.** This Agreement becomes effective on the later of:
   - the date and time the Company comes into existence upon filing of its Certificate of Formation with the Registrar; and
   - any later effective date stated in the Certificate of Formation or this Agreement.

3. **Members.** Each person or entity admitted as a Member under this Agreement is a party to this Agreement. No person becomes a Member except as provided herein or as mandatory law requires. The Members collectively and the Company are the parties to this Agreement.

4. **Algorithmic Management.** The Company is an algorithmically managed decentralized autonomous organization within the meaning of the DAO Act. The Governance System and any smart contracts identified in the Smart Contract Registry may be used to manage or facilitate Company activities, subject to this Agreement and the Certificate of Formation.

   Algorithmic management includes the on-chain adoption of Resolutions within the Governance System and the execution of Execution Bundles that effectuate such Resolutions. The mere fact that a Resolution interacts with external on-chain programs or accounts does not make those external systems part of the Governance System or subject them to Company control. External programs and accounts are encompassed only if expressly designated by Resolution and recorded in the Smart Contract Registry.

### 1.2 Registered Office, Registered Agent, and Representative Agent

The Company's registered office and registered agent in the Republic of the Marshall Islands shall be MIDAO Directory Services (or any successor registered agent permitted under the DAO Act), at the address stated in the Certificate of Formation.

The Company shall designate and continuously maintain a Representative Agent as required by the DAO Act and DAO Regulations. The Representative Agent is an administrative and statutory contact role and does not, by itself, confer authority over the Governance System, Company treasury, or any deployment of Protocol IP.

The Representative Agent shall prepare, execute, and file the Beneficial Owner Information Report (Form 2) and any annual filings (Form 4) required under DAO Act §112 and the DAO Regulations. For purposes of those filings, the Representative Agent shall also serve as the Company's Senior Managing Official (SMO). Where BOIR/Form 2 Category C applies, the Head Steward, in the limited role set out under Section 8.4(1), also serves as the Company's authorized representative member for BOIR purposes in accordance with Section 8.4(2). The Representative Agent (including in its SMO capacity) has a narrow statutory-compliance mandate only.

### 1.3 No Partnership or Agency

The Members intend that the Company be treated as a limited liability company under the DAO Act and LLC Act, not as a general partnership or limited partnership. Except as expressly provided in this Agreement or in a separate written contract approved by the Governance System:

- no Member is a partner, joint venturer, or agent of any other Member or of the Company; and
- no Member has authority to bind the Company or any other Member merely by reason of holding Tokens or Membership Interests.

**Member-to-member dealings.** Members remain free to transact and associate with one another in their personal capacities. Limitations on Member conduct apply only to Company affairs and are set out in the Founding Charter. With respect to Company matters, Members must act consistently with the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing.

## Article II -- Definitions and Interpretation

### 2.1 Definitions

In this Agreement:

1. **"Blockchain"** means a distributed ledger maintained by a decentralized network of computers using cryptographic methods to validate and record transactions. **"Blockchain System"** means the Solana mainnet blockchain unless otherwise specified. **"Smart Contract"** means self-executing code deployed on a Blockchain that executes according to its programmed logic.

2. **"Company Treasury Accounts"** means only those Solana accounts, smart contracts, or addresses that:
   - are designated by Resolution as holding property in which the Company holds all right, title, and interest; and
   - are listed in the Smart Contract Registry.

   Company Treasury Accounts exclude all accounts holding assets belonging to third parties. The Governance System's technical ability to propose or execute transactions touching any account does not, by itself, make that account a Company Treasury Account or its contents property of the Company.

3. **"Smart Contract Registry"** means the registry forming part of this Agreement that, for purposes of DAO Act §106(2), identifies the publicly available identifiers of smart contracts directly used to manage or facilitate the Company's governance. The Smart Contract Registry includes a Company Treasury Accounts Register identifying addresses designated as Company property. The Smart Contract Registry may be updated only in accordance with Sections 6.6 and 6.7.

4. **"Website Registry"** means the registry forming part of this Agreement that, for purposes of DAO Act §102(p), designates the Company's official online venues where Members convene to participate in governance. The Website Registry may be updated only in accordance with Sections 6.6 and 6.7.

5. **"Intellectual Property Registry"** means the registry forming part of this Agreement that identifies the Protocol IP of the Company and records, for reference and provenance purposes, the canonical instruments, artefacts, and identifiers comprising Protocol IP, together with any updates adopted in accordance with the Founding Charter and recorded in the SOP Registry.

6. **"IP License & Assignment Register"** means the register forming part of this Agreement that serves as the canonical index of Licenses granted by the Company in respect of Protocol IP and any related assignments to the Company recorded under the IP License Framework, including references (by identifier, path, and/or hash commitment) to the then-current license package and any schedules, as updated from time to time in accordance with the Founding Charter and recorded in the SOP Registry.

7. **"Counterparty Agreements Register"** means the register forming part of this Agreement that serves as the canonical index of agreements the Company enters into with external parties (including amendments, statements of work, side letters, and terminations). Recording in the Counterparty Agreements Register is for governance traceability and record-keeping only. An agreement recorded in the Counterparty Agreements Register does not amend, supplement, or modify any license of Protocol IP, the IP License Framework, or any entry in the Intellectual Property Registry or IP License & Assignment Register, except where the Company has expressly adopted such amendment in the manner required by the Company Agreement and recorded it in the SOP Registry.

8. **"SOP Registry"** means the Company's canonical record repository designated under the Company Agreement for maintaining adopted instruments, registries, registers, and SOP artefacts (including public records, hash commitments, and governance record packages), as updated from time to time in accordance with the Founding Charter.

9. **"Execution Bundle"** means the on-chain transaction or transactions associated with a governance proposal that implement a Resolution, as further described in the Founding Charter.

10. **"MetaDAO"** means MetaDAO LLC, a Republic of the Marshall Islands limited liability company, which holds intellectual property rights in futarchy-based governance mechanisms. The Company's utilization of MetaDAO intellectual property is predicated on the reasonable expectation that MetaDAO maintains appropriate legal arrangements with respect to such intellectual property and that any authorized operator or implementer of such intellectual property maintains compliance with applicable laws in relevant jurisdictions, without any representation, warranty, or undertaking by the Company as to any of the foregoing and without any duty by the Company to verify the same.

11. **"Futarchic Mechanism"** means the smart contracts on Solana implementing MetaDAO's futarchy-based governance intellectual property (Futarchy), as designated by the Company as the primary market-based governance mechanism for adoption or rejection of proposals, including any successors or upgrades. The Company participates in the Futarchic Mechanism as a user and does not operate, maintain, or have administrative authority over such smart contracts.

12. **"Governance System"** means, collectively:
   - the Futarchic Mechanism;
   - any voting, proposal, or execution coordinators used by the Company; and
   - any Squads or equivalent on-chain multisig used to originate Execution Bundles;

   as further described in the Founding Charter.

13. **"Proposal Memoranda"** means governance proposal documents maintained in the Company's public proposal repository, conforming to the adopted record schema. Proposal Memoranda are for transparency and best practice only and are not mechanically binding on the Governance System.

14. **"Membership Tokens"** means the token(s) on the Blockchain System that the Company Agreement designates as conferring voting or economic rights in the Company and representing Membership Interests, as identified by mint address(es) and any successor identifiers recorded in the Smart Contract Registry.

15. **"Membership Interest"** means a Member's personal contractual rights under this Agreement, including economic and voting rights (if any) and the covenant of good faith and fair dealing. A Membership Interest is a contractual right only. Membership Interests are represented by Membership Tokens and transfer with the corresponding Membership Tokens on the Blockchain System.

    Holding or administering Tokens that are not Membership Tokens does not, by itself, make the holder a Member or confer any Membership Interest. Membership rights arise only from the Company Agreement.

16. **"Implementation"** means any deployment, instantiation, copy, interface, integration, or operational stack (whether on-chain or off-chain) that implements, embodies, executes, uses, exposes, or interfaces with any Protocol IP, including on-chain programs, upgrade authorities, multisigs, signers, frontends, APIs, SDKs, bots, keepers, relayers, indexers, data services, servers, websites, repositories, and any other infrastructure or runtime environment.

    An Implementation is not the Company, is not operated by the Company, and is not attributable to the Company for any purpose (including operation, control, custody, issuance, administration, maintenance, compliance responsibility, or regulatory characterization of the Company), regardless of:
    - any governance activity, Resolution, vote, parameter setting, publication, listing, specification, recommendation, or documentation by the Company;
    - any IP ownership or licensing by the Company; or
    - any participation by Members in their individual capacities.

    No Implementation is deemed Company-operated or Company-controlled by virtue of the Company describing it, referencing it, licensing Protocol IP to it, receiving reports about it, approving a Resolution that contemplates it, or Members voting for or against actions relating to it.

17. **"Protocol IP"** means the intellectual property defined in the Founding Charter and recorded in the Intellectual Property Registry, including this Agreement and other governance instruments. "Protocol" is shorthand for Protocol IP only.

    Protocol IP is intangible. In relation to software, Protocol IP includes (to the maximum extent protectable) source code, compiled and object code, bytecode, and formal abstractions and specifications (including interfaces, IDLs, ABIs, state machines, schemas, models, proofs, and abstract syntax trees), whether expressed in repositories or embodied in fixed copies (referring solely to program code as recorded in blockchain state, not to runtime execution, transaction processing, or state transitions resulting from invocation). In relation to governance, Protocol IP includes the Company Agreement and all instruments incorporated by reference.

Protocol IP does not include, and must not be construed as, any operational activity, operational role, or operational control in any form. Protocol IP does not include ownership of, custody over, or responsibility for: any runtime execution, transaction flow, order flow, validator activity, block production, network operation, hosting, key management, upgrade authority, admin privileges, onboarding, support, compliance operations, or any assets held by, transacting with, or associated with any Implementation.

Such listing must not be interpreted as asserting that the Company operates, controls, administers, maintains, manages, supports, or provides any Implementation, or holds any keys, credentials, or operational privileges, or is responsible for any outcomes, transactions, or assets associated with it.

    "Protocol" is not and must not be interpreted as an operating service, issuer, custodian, counterparty, partnership, joint venture, agent, fiduciary, manager, or other person or entity, and must not be construed to imply operation or control by the Company.

    References in this Agreement to "operate," "run," "deploy," "upgrade," "administer," "maintain," "manage," "rebalance," "mint," "burn," "issue," "redeem," or similar verbs, when used in connection with Protocol IP, refer only to software capabilities, design descriptions, or IP artefacts, and not to any activity by the Company.

19. **"Willful Misconduct"** means any deliberate act or omission that constitutes either Fraud or a knowing and intentional disregard of material duties under the Company Agreement or mandatory law, where such an act or omission is reasonably expected to cause material harm to the Company. Willful Misconduct does not include negligence, errors of judgment, or mistakes made in good faith.

20. **"Primary Instruments"** means this Agreement, the Founding Charter, and the IP License Framework.

21. **"Secondary Instruments"** means the Smart Contract Registry, Website Registry, Intellectual Property Registry, IP License & Assignment Register, and the Counterparty Agreements Register.

## Article III -- Purpose and Powers

### 3.1 Purpose

The Company is organized as a for-profit DAO LLC. The purposes of the Company are to:

1. hold, steward, and license Protocol IP;

2. manage the Company's governance through the Governance System, including adopting and amending governance instruments, configuring parameters and treasury allocations, and appointing persons to carry out delegated functions;

3. manage Company Treasury Accounts and any assets in which the Company holds all right, title, and interest; and

4. execute all other lawful acts necessary, desirable, or incidental to the foregoing purposes.

### 3.2 Powers

Subject to the DAO Act, LLC Act, this Agreement, and valid Resolutions, the Company has the power to:

1. acquire, hold, dispose of, and otherwise deal with assets in Company Treasury Accounts;

2. grant and enforce licenses of Protocol IP;

3. enter into agreements on behalf of the Company;

4. adopt, alter, and use a Company seal, and to cause it or a facsimile (including an electronic or digital reproduction) to be affixed, impressed, or reproduced in any manner on any instrument, certificate, or record of the Company;

5. indemnify persons as permitted herein and under Marshall Islands law; and

6. take any other actions authorized by valid Resolution.

### 3.3 Execution of Agreements

The Head Steward may execute agreements within the scope of their role as Representative Agent and Senior Managing Official. Subcommittees established under the Founding Charter may execute agreements within the scope of their delegated authority. A cryptographic signature recorded when a person with proper authority signs or approves a blockchain transaction satisfies any requirement for a written signature pursuant to the DAO Act.

Authority to bind the Company is limited to:
- agreements approved by Resolution;
- agreements necessary to fulfill statutory filing and compliance obligations; and
- agreements within the scope of any delegation recorded in the SOP Registry.

No agreement executed under this Section creates any agency, partnership, or joint venture between the Company and any counterparty, or any read-through of the counterparty's activities to the Company, unless the agreement expressly states otherwise and such relationship is approved by Resolution.

### 3.4 Dissolution

1. The Governance System may, by Resolution, approve an orderly wind-down or dissolution of the Company, including liquidation of assets in Company Treasury Accounts, as permitted by this Agreement and applicable law.

2. Only assets in which the Company holds all right, title, and interest may be treated as available for liquidation or distribution.

3. Any distribution of Company assets on dissolution, including Protocol IP or proceeds thereof, must be calculated on a fully-diluted basis inclusive of all Membership Interests, whether or not subject to lockup or transfer restriction.

4. Any Resolution that affects a dissolution inconsistent with this Section 3.4 is ultra vires to the extent of the inconsistency.

## Article IV -- Members, Membership Interests, and Limited Liability

### 4.1 Members and Membership Interests

1. A **Member** is any person admitted as a member under this Agreement, including:
   - any person who holds tokens or other property that confers a voting or economic right in the Company, in accordance with DAO Act §106; and
   - where applicable, the Head Steward solely in the limited capacity of an authorized representative member under Section 8.4(2).

2. A person ceases to be a Member:
   - in the case of a Member whose status arises from tokens or other property conferring voting or economic rights, when they transfer, sell, or alienate all such tokens or property and retain no further right of ownership therein; and
   - in the case of the Head Steward serving as an authorized representative member under Section 8.4(2), upon cessation of the Head Steward role or when Category C is no longer applicable.

3. A **Membership Interest** is a personal contractual right and does not represent a share of corporate stock, partnership interest, or security under any jurisdiction, unless required by that jurisdiction's mandatory law.

   Characterization of Membership Interests or Membership Tokens under the laws of the Republic of the Marshall Islands is provided solely for purposes of this Agreement and is not a representation regarding characterization under the laws of any other jurisdiction. The Company makes no representation or warranty that Membership Tokens are not securities, commodities, financial products, or similarly regulated instruments outside the Republic of the Marshall Islands.

4. Membership and Membership Interests are determined by token holdings as recorded on the Blockchain System. The Company is not required to maintain a separate register of Members.

### 4.2 Limited Liability

To the fullest extent permitted by the DAO Act and LLC Act:

1. No Member shall be personally liable for any debts, obligations, or liabilities of the Company solely by reason of being a Member.

2. Creditors of the Company have recourse only to assets held in Company Treasury Accounts, and not to the separate assets of any Member, except where a separate written agreement expressly provides otherwise.

### 4.3 Covenant of Good Faith

1. Subject to mandatory law, the Members agree that their primary duty to the Company and each other is the contractual covenant of good faith and fair dealing (DAO Act §109), not a generalized fiduciary, partnership, or agency duty.

2. Each Member agrees to exercise governance and proposal rights in good faith and with a primary intent to benefit the Company, rather than to expropriate or deliberately harm other participants.

3. Nothing in this Agreement prevents a Member from pursuing independent business opportunities unrelated to the Company, provided that they do not misuse Confidential Information or assets of the Company. This Section does not grant any license to Protocol IP.

4. No Member has authority to bind, represent, or speak on behalf of the Company solely by reason of being a Member. Any representation that a Member is acting on behalf of the Company without authorization by Resolution and by the Head Steward is unauthorized and does not bind the Company.

### 4.4 No Obligation to Violate Law

No Member or Head Steward shall be obligated under this Agreement to implement or comply with any Resolution or instruction that would result in a violation of applicable mandatory law in any jurisdiction to which such person is subject.

### 4.5 Excluded Persons and Prohibited Property

1. The following persons are not eligible to be Members and any purported Membership Interest held by such person is void:

   - any person subject to sanctions administered by the United States Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC), the United Nations Security Council, the European Union, or the United Kingdom;
   - any person identified on a sanctions list maintained by the Republic of the Marshall Islands or notified to the Company by the Registrar;
   - any politically exposed person where, in the reasonable determination of the Head Steward, such person's membership would expose the Company or other Members to material legal, regulatory, or reputational risk; and
   - any other person whose membership would, in the reasonable determination of the Head Steward, cause the Company or other Members to be subject to extraterritorial legal action, sanctions, or enforcement.

2. The Company shall not accept capital contributions, distributions, or any transfer of property from or to any Excluded Person. Any such transfer is void and must be reversed or blocked to the extent technically feasible.

3. If a Member becomes an Excluded Person after admission, their Membership Interest is automatically suspended. The Head Steward shall take reasonable steps to effect dissociation and prevent further participation by such person.

4. The Head Steward may maintain a non-public register of Excluded Persons for compliance purposes. Such register is Confidential Information.

5. No Member, the Head Steward, or the Company shall be liable for good-faith determinations under this Section, even if a determination is later found to be incorrect.

6. The Company shall not accept any property that is, as determined by the Head Steward:
   - stolen, misappropriated, or the proceeds of unlawful activity;
   - subject to seizure, forfeiture, or legal dispute;
   - tainted by association with sanctioned persons, illicit markets, or money laundering; or
   - otherwise likely to expose the Company or other Members to legal, regulatory, or reputational risk.

   Any purported transfer of such property to the Company is void. The Head Steward may take reasonable steps to block, reverse, or quarantine any such property to the extent technically feasible.

7. For purposes of accepting capital contributions, administering any Company-recognized claim process, implementing Company-facilitated distributions, or carrying out any Company-facing treasury interaction, the Head Steward and/or any Subcommittee acting within its mandate under the Founding Charter (including the Company Treasury Subcommittee, where applicable) may require reasonable compliance conditions, including identity verification, sanctions screening, beneficial-ownership information, source-of-funds or source-of-wealth information, tax documentation, jurisdictional or residency attestations, wallet screening, proof of control of receiving addresses, and use of designated submission procedures.

8. If a person fails or refuses to satisfy a compliance condition reasonably imposed under this Agreement or required by applicable law, the Company, acting through the Head Steward and/or a duly authorized Subcommittee, may, to the extent reasonably necessary and technically feasible reject, defer, suspend, quarantine, return, or route the relevant contribution, claim, or Company-facilitated distribution through an approved lawful process or intermediary. Any such action taken in good faith for compliance or risk-management purposes is not a breach of this Agreement.

9. The Company may, by Resolution or where required by applicable law, adopt additional compliance mechanics for future Company-facing interactions, including geofencing, attestations, whitelisting, Company-recognition and claim gating, claim procedures, deadlines, Company-interface access controls, or designated intermediaries for distributions or treasury interactions. For avoidance of doubt, such mechanics govern Company-facing and Company-facilitated processes and do not, by themselves, imply direct technical control over autonomous on-chain transfers or independent third-party programs. No Member is entitled to any particular distribution channel, settlement rail, wallet, interface, or timing except as expressly provided in a Distribution Resolution and subject to this Agreement and mandatory law.

10. No prior acceptance of a contribution, no prior distribution practice, no prior claim practice, and no historical absence of screening, geofencing, or documentation requirements creates a waiver, estoppel, or continuing obligation to maintain the same process for future transactions or distributions.

## Article V -- Capital and Company Property

### 5.1 Capital Contributions

1. The Company may accept capital contributions in the form and amount determined by Resolution.

2. A Member is not obligated to make additional capital contributions unless agreed in writing and approved by Resolution.

3. No Member is entitled to interest on any capital contribution.

### 5.2 Company Property

Company Property consists solely of:

1. assets held in Company Treasury Accounts;

2. Protocol IP and related rights assigned or licensed to the Company; and

3. any claims, rights, or receivables held by the Company under written agreements.

The Company's powers extend only to Company Property. The Company does not own, custody, or control any assets solely by reason of those assets interacting with any Implementation.

### 5.3 Distributions

1. The Company's initial treasury was contributed through the token launch process, in exchange for which contributors received tokens conferring Membership Interests. Such contributions and the issuance of Membership Interests are governed by the DAO Act and are not subject to securities laws of the Republic of the Marshall Islands.

2. Company Property may be augmented by license fees, revenues from the Company's activities as principal with its own assets, and any other lawful sources. The Company acts as principal in all treasury activities and does not act as agent, custodian, or service provider.

3. Distributions, if any, may only be made from Company Property, subject to solvency requirements under applicable law.

4. Distributions are made only pursuant to Resolution and, where applicable, execution of the relevant Execution Bundle.

5. A Resolution approving a distribution (a **"Distribution Resolution"**) may specify eligibility conditions, timing, procedures, claim mechanics, deadlines, reserves, withholding, staged settlement, designated intermediaries, designated receiving wallets, documentation requirements, and other operational or compliance conditions for that distribution event, provided such terms are not inconsistent with mandatory law or the protected provisions of this Agreement.

6. The Company may condition any Company-facilitated distribution on compliance requirements reasonably imposed under this Agreement, applicable law, or a Distribution Resolution, including identity verification, sanctions screening, beneficial-ownership information, tax forms, source-of-funds information, jurisdictional or residency attestations, wallet screening, and proof of control of receiving addresses.

7. If a Member or claimant fails to satisfy applicable distribution conditions, the Company, acting through the Head Steward and/or a duly authorized Subcommittee (including the Company Treasury Subcommittee where applicable), may defer, withhold, suspend, quarantine, return, or otherwise not complete that Company-facilitated distribution to such person unless and until the conditions are satisfied or the Distribution Resolution provides another lawful outcome. A good-faith compliance hold, delay, or refusal under this Section is not a breach of this Agreement and does not create any personal liability for the Head Steward or any Subcommittee Member acting within scope.

   For avoidance of doubt, this Section governs Company-recognized or Company-facilitated distribution processes and does not create any implication that the Head Steward may unilaterally revoke, amend, or override autonomous on-chain execution by the Governance System or by independent third-party programs outside the Company's direct control.

8. No Member has a fixed right to any distribution, to any recurring distribution program, or to any particular distribution channel or settlement timing. The Company may retain assets to build reserves.

9. Any distribution may have tax, accounting, or reporting consequences. Each Member is responsible for their own compliance.

Company compliance mechanics govern only Company-facing recognition, claim handling, and Company-facilitated settlement processes, and do not invalidate or reverse autonomous on-chain state changes outside the Company's control.

## Article VI -- Governance and Execution Bundles

### 6.1 Governance System and Resolutions

1. The Governance System (Futarchy) is the primary mechanism by which the Company adopts Resolutions and directs the use of Company Treasury Accounts.

2. A Resolution is valid only if:
   - it is adopted according to the rules set out in the Founding Charter and any subsequently adopted governance instruments; and
   - any required Execution Bundle is executed on Solana.

3. To the extent permitted by law, the acts taken under a valid Execution Bundle are treated as acts of the Company.

### 6.2 Member Responsibilities Regarding Execution Bundles

Each Member is expected to review, or cause to be reviewed, the Execution Bundles associated with proposals they support, including deserialized instructions and address lookup tables. Reliance on high-level summaries does not override Execution Bundles.

### 6.3 Amendments to Governance Rules

1. The Company may adopt or amend governance procedures via Resolution, provided such rules are consistent with this Agreement and applicable law.

2. In case of conflict, this Agreement and the Founding Charter override any lower-level governance documentation.

### 6.4 Governance Continuity

If the Futarchic Mechanism or any component of the Governance System becomes unavailable, non-functional, legally impermissible, or otherwise unsuitable for continued use, the Company may by Resolution designate a successor mechanism or system that substantially preserves the market-based governance principles set forth in this Agreement and the Founding Charter. Such designation shall not constitute an amendment to this Agreement, provided the successor mechanism operates consistently with the spirit and intent of the original Governance System.

### 6.5 Amendments to the Company Agreement

1. This Agreement and other Primary Instruments may be amended only by Resolution that:

## Article VII -- Dissociation and Transfer of Interests

### 7.1 Dissociation

1. A Member dissociates from the Company when they transfer, sell, or alienate all tokens that confer voting or economic rights in the Company and retain no further ownership, in accordance with DAO Act §106.

2. A Member may also be dissociated by Resolution if they become an Excluded Person under Section 4.5.

3. On dissociation, the former Member:
   - ceases to hold any Membership Interest;
   - retains any tokens they hold in their own wallets (subject to on-chain program logic); and
   - remains bound by any confidentiality or non-circumvention obligations incurred while a Member.

### 7.2 Transfers

1. Membership Interests are represented by tokens and transfer with the tokens on the Blockchain System.

2. Any transfer to an Excluded Person is void and does not make the transferee a Member.

### 7.3 Company Sale

1. For purposes of this Section, **"Voting Supply"** means all tokens conferring voting rights in the Company, excluding:
   - tokens subject to lockup or transfer restriction that cannot be exercised at the relevant time; and
   - tokens held in Company Treasury Accounts.

2. A **"Company Sale"** means any transaction or series of transactions that results in:
   - a transfer of more than fifty percent (50%) of the Voting Supply;
   - a merger, consolidation, or reorganization in which the Members immediately prior do not retain majority control; or
   - a sale, exclusive license, or disposition of all or substantially all Protocol IP.

3. Any distribution of Company property on a Company Sale, including Protocol IP or proceeds thereof, must be calculated on a fully-diluted basis inclusive of all tokens, whether or not subject to lockup or transfer restriction, but excluding tokens held in Company Treasury Accounts.

4. A Company Sale shall not be used to exclude tokens subject to lockup from economic participation. Any Resolution effecting a Company Sale that fails to account for all tokens on a fully-diluted basis is beyond the scope of the Company's authority.

5. Allocation of sale consideration is a mechanical calculation only. It does not create any trust, fiduciary arrangement, or equitable interest. It does not create fiduciary duties, duties of care, or duties to maximize sale price.

6. No purchaser or successor assumes any obligation under the Company Agreement unless expressly stated in the definitive sale documents.
