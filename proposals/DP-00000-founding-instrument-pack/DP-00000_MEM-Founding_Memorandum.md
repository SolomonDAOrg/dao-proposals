## Scope and Legal Posture

This Memorandum is incorporated into the Company Agreement. It provides background and context only. Operational actions require post-formation Resolutions.

### Intellectual Property Firewall

1. **"Protocol"** in this Memorandum has the same meaning as **"Protocol IP"** as defined in Operating Agreement §2.1(17) - design, documentation, code, repositories, and other intellectual property. It does not mean an operating business, service, issuer, custodian, counterparty, or other person or entity.

2. Protocol IP is licensed under terms that require licensees to comply with applicable law and assume responsibility for their own deployments and operations. The Company does not operate, control, administer, or maintain any deployment of Protocol IP and shall not be deemed to do so. Licensee conduct is independent and is not attributable to the Company.

3. Adoption of this Company Agreement or any Resolution does not obligate the Company to operate, provide, maintain, or support any deployment or service. No such obligation should be inferred from treasury directions or governance documentation.

### Protocol IP Scope

**"Protocol IP"** has the meaning given in the Operating Agreement §2.1(17). For convenience, it includes (to the maximum extent protectable):
- source code;
- compiled, object, and bytecode;
- formal abstractions and specifications (including interfaces, IDLs, ABIs, state machines, schemas, and abstract syntax trees); and
- expressions in repositories or embodied in deployed copies.

Protocol IP does not include ownership of or operational control over any runtime execution, transaction flow, validator activity, block production, or assets held by or transacting with any deployment.

The schedule of Protocol IP is maintained in the Intellectual Property Registry.
