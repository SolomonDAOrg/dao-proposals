# Founding Charter of SOLOMON DAO LLC

**Status:** Pre-formation (executed by Organizer; not adopted by on-chain vote)

**Effectiveness:** This Charter takes effect on the Effective Date of the Company's Certificate of Formation.

**PRIMARY INSTRUMENT.** This Founding Charter is a binding primary instrument of the Company Agreement and governs member rights, Subcommittees, oversight mechanisms, and delegated authority.

**Not the Registrar-issued Corporate Charter.** This Founding Charter is an internal governance instrument. It is not the Registrar-issued Corporate Charter that accompanies the Certificate of Formation under DAO Act §717 and the DAO Regulations, and does not replace or purport to be that Registrar-issued instrument.

---

Anyone acquiring Membership Interests must carefully review the Company Agreement in full and consider obtaining independent legal or professional advice.

## Article I -- Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Purpose

The purpose of the Company is to:

1. hold, steward, protect, and license Protocol IP;
2. adopt and maintain internal governance instruments and registries;
3. direct the use of Company Treasury Accounts through the Governance System; and
4. establish member rights, Subcommittees, oversight mechanisms, and delegated authority.

### 1.2 Intellectual Property Firewall

1. **"Protocol"** in this Charter has the same meaning as **"Protocol IP"** as defined in Operating Agreement §2.1(17) - design, documentation, code, repositories, and other intellectual property. It does not mean an operating business, service, issuer, custodian, counterparty, or other person or entity.

2. Protocol IP is licensed under terms that require licensees to comply with applicable law and assume responsibility for their own deployments and operations. The Company does not operate, control, administer, or maintain any deployment of Protocol IP and shall not be deemed to do so. Licensee conduct is independent and is not attributable to the Company.

3. Adoption of this Charter or any Resolution does not obligate the Company to operate, provide, maintain, or support any deployment or service. No such obligation should be inferred from treasury directions or governance documentation.

### 1.3 Scope of this Charter

This Charter:

1. supplements member rights and participation established in the Operating Agreement;
2. establishes Subcommittees and their authority;
3. establishes oversight and transparency mechanisms; and
4. sets standards of conduct for persons exercising authority under the Company Agreement.

This Charter is part of the Company Agreement and must be interpreted as a whole with the other instruments of the Company Agreement.

### 1.4 Member Rights in Protocol IP

Protocol IP is Company Property. Members benefit from Protocol IP through their Membership Interests. No Member has any individual right to use, license, or dispose of Protocol IP except as expressly provided by Resolution or under the IP License Framework.

## Article II -- Definitions

Terms defined in the Operating Agreement have the same meaning in this Charter unless otherwise specified.

### 2.1 Subcommittee

**"Subcommittee"** has the meaning given in the Operating Agreement. For clarity, Subcommittees include Subcommittees established by this Charter and Subcommittees established by Resolution under Article IV.

### 2.2 Subcommittee Member

A **"Subcommittee Member"** is a person appointed to serve on a Subcommittee. Only Members may serve on Subcommittees unless this Charter or a Resolution expressly permits otherwise.

### 2.3 Authority

The Head Steward and each Subcommittee exercise only the authority granted by the Operating Agreement, this Charter, and applicable Resolutions.

The Head Steward is authorized to act within the scope of Section 3.3 of the Operating Agreement.

Each Subcommittee is authorized to act only within its defined scope. Subcommittee composition and operational details are Confidential Information, except to the extent disclosure is required by mandatory law.

### 2.4 Oversight Mechanism

An **"Oversight Mechanism"** is a procedure established by this Charter or by Resolution to review, audit, or verify the activities of persons exercising authority, including Audit Subcommittees and confidential review procedures.

## Article III -- Member Conduct

### 3.1 Member Rights

Member rights are established in the Operating Agreement. This Charter supplements but does not limit those rights.

### 3.2 Good Faith Covenant

Members acknowledge that their core duty to the Company is the contractual covenant of good faith and fair dealing, and not any broader fiduciary duty, partnership, or agency obligation.

### 3.3 Governance Integrity

Each Member agrees to exercise governance rights in good faith and with a primary intent to benefit the Company, rather than:

1. expropriating, harassing, or deliberately harming other Members; or
2. attempting to coerce or improperly influence persons exercising authority outside the mechanisms defined in the Company Agreement.

### 3.4 Bad Faith

Disagreement over policy or direction is not bad faith. Bad faith includes:

1. using governance to pursue personal vendettas or to deliberately impair the economic value or goodwill of Protocol IP;
2. knowingly proposing or supporting actions that would violate mandatory law;
3. intentionally misrepresenting the meaning or effect of a proposal;
4. proposing or supporting governance actions whose primary purpose is to promote unlawful conduct or non-compliance with mandatory legal obligations; or
5. engaging in harassment, spam, or knowingly false statements in governance venues where the primary purpose is to coerce outcomes or damage reputation.

### 3.5 Exit, Not Sabotage

Membership is voluntary. A Member who no longer wishes to be bound by the Company's decisions may dissociate in accordance with the Operating Agreement. Exit, not sabotage, is the expected remedy for irreconcilable disagreement.

### 3.6 Fairness is Policy Guidance

Any references in the Company Agreement to fairness, parity, or non-discrimination are policy guidance only. Such references do not create fiduciary duties and do not create any trust, constructive trust, or equitable ownership by implication.

### 3.7 Member Sophistication

Each Member acknowledges that:

1. blockchain systems are complex and rapidly evolving;
2. participation requires sufficient understanding to make informed decisions about risk;
3. the Company is not intended for low-information or passive participants;
4. each Member is responsible for monitoring changes to Protocol IP and the surrounding ecosystem; and
5. the Company does not undertake to keep any Member informed or to ensure any Member's knowledge remains adequate.

### 3.8 No Suitability Obligation

The Company has no obligation to determine whether participation is suitable for any particular person. Participation is undertaken at the Member's own risk and expense.

### 3.9 No Advice

The Company does not and shall not provide professional advice or services as set out in Section 8.2 of the Operating Agreement. Each Member is solely responsible for obtaining independent advice and for complying with all laws applicable to them.

### 3.10 Minimum Age and Legal Capacity

Participation is intended only for persons who have reached the age of majority and have full legal capacity under the laws of their jurisdiction. No natural person under eighteen (18) years of age may participate. By acquiring Membership Interests, each natural person represents that they:

1. are at least eighteen (18) years old; and
2. have full legal capacity to enter into binding arrangements.

### 3.11 Ultra Vires

Proposals and governance actions clearly inconsistent with this Article may be treated as ultra vires and subject to challenge, delay, or non-implementation.

## Article IV -- Subcommittees

### 4.1 Establishment of Subcommittees

The Company may establish Subcommittees by Resolution to exercise authority within a defined scope. Each Resolution establishing a Subcommittee shall specify:

1. the scope of the Subcommittee's authority;
2. the composition and appointment mechanism;
3. any limits on the Subcommittee's authority; and
4. the mechanism for removal or dissolution of the Subcommittee.

### 4.2 Subcommittee Membership

Only Members may serve on Subcommittees unless a Resolution expressly permits otherwise.

Subcommittee Members serve until they resign, are removed by Resolution, or cease to satisfy the eligibility requirements applicable to their appointment.

### 4.3 Scope Limits

A Subcommittee may act only within the scope specified in the Resolution establishing it and any scope expressly granted by this Agreement for that Subcommittee. Any action outside that scope is beyond the authority of the Subcommittee and does not bind the Company.

### 4.4 Company Treasury Subcommittee

The Company Treasury Subcommittee is authorized to manage Company Treasury Accounts in the ordinary course of business, subject to policies and limits adopted by Resolution.

For purposes of this Agreement, "**ordinary course of treasury management**" includes, when performed subject to policies and limits adopted by Resolution:

1. maintaining and transacting from Company Treasury Accounts, including establishing, controlling, and administering wallets, accounts, custody solutions (if any), and settlement rails;
2. implementing treasury policies adopted by Resolution;
3. recommending treasury policies and parameters for adoption by Resolution;
4. entering into, administering, and enforcing treasury operational arrangements that are ancillary to treasury management, including (as applicable) minting, burning, reserve, redemption, and settlement frameworks relating to Company Treasury Assets, and related execution, market-making, liquidity provisioning, settlement, payment, and account services, provided such arrangements are within limits adopted by Resolution; and
5. approving and administering constrained technical access arrangements required to interact with on-chain programs in furtherance of treasury management, including role assignments, allowlists, caps, revocation procedures, and operator appointments, in each case revocable and subject to parameter limits adopted by Resolution.

Any third-party access must be structured to avoid granting unilateral control over Company Treasury Assets or unilateral authority to mint or burn against Company Treasury Assets, and must be revocable.

**Preference for on-chain multisig controls.** Where feasible, the Treasury Subcommittee shall implement treasury control and authorization through on-chain multisignature arrangements (including, as applicable, Squads or similar multisig frameworks) and/or programmatic controls that require multiple approvals for sensitive actions. The purpose of such controls is to (i) preserve Company control over Company Treasury Assets, (ii) prevent any single person or third-party service provider from exercising unilateral control, and (iii) mechanically constrain delegated operational roles (including market makers and other operators) to permitted actions without granting custody or discretionary transfer authority.

**Protocol IP / operations separation**. The Company's ownership or licensing of Protocol IP does not, by itself, mean the Company operates any minting, trading, or settlement services. The Treasury Subcommittee must exercise its authority so that independent third-party service providers act as independent contractors and remain responsible for their own operations and compliance programs; Company approvals are limited to treasury constraints (including permitted assets, caps, allowlists, and revocation) and do not constitute operation of such services by the Company.

The Company Treasury Subcommittee is not authorized to:

1. act outside policies or limits adopted by Resolution;
2. expand its own authority;
3. bind the Company to obligations not reasonably incidental to ordinary course treasury management as described above, or outside limits adopted by Resolution; or
4. engage in self-dealing or transact for personal benefit.

### 4.5 Audit Subcommittee

The Company may establish an Audit Subcommittee by Resolution to:

1. review the activities of persons exercising authority under the Company Agreement;
2. engage independent auditors or reviewers;
3. receive and review confidential information under appropriate controls; and
4. report findings to Members.

An Audit Subcommittee operates under confidentiality obligations established by the Resolution appointing it.

No Subcommittee may audit or review its own activities. The Audit Subcommittee may review other Subcommittees, and other Subcommittees or independent reviewers appointed by Resolution may review the Audit Subcommittee.

### 4.6 Other Subcommittees

The Company may establish other Subcommittees by Resolution for functions consistent with the Company's purpose, including:

1. grants and ecosystem programs supporting Protocol IP adoption;
2. research and development of Protocol IP;
3. stewardship of Protocol IP; and
4. other functions advancing the Company's interests in Protocol IP.

### 4.7 Standard of Conduct

Subcommittee Members shall exercise authority in good faith within the scope of their appointment.

Subcommittee Members are not liable for losses arising from good faith actions within scope, except in cases of Fraud or Willful Misconduct.

### 4.8 Confidentiality

Subcommittee composition and operational details are Confidential Information, except to the extent disclosure is required by mandatory law.

## Article V -- Oversight and Transparency

### 5.1 Record Retention

The Company shall retain governance, licensing, and registry records for at least five (5) years in accordance with mandatory law.

### 5.2 Independent Review

The Company may engage independent auditors or reviewers by Resolution to:

1. audit Protocol IP, including smart contracts and related code;
2. verify that treasury activities and controls are conducted in accordance with policies adopted by Resolution and for the benefit of the Company;
3. verify compliance with the Company Agreement; and
4. report findings to Members or to a designated Subcommittee.

### 5.3 Confidential Review

Where a matter requires review but public disclosure would compromise security or confidentiality, the Company may by Resolution appoint a confidential review Subcommittee or engage an independent reviewer under confidentiality obligations.

A confidential review Subcommittee may receive and review Confidential Information and Security-Sensitive Information. Findings may be reported in summary form that does not disclose Confidential Information or Security-Sensitive Information.

### 5.4 Review Does Not Waive Confidentiality

Nothing in this Article supersedes Article IX of the Operating Agreement.

## Article VI -- Standards of Conduct

### 6.1 Good Faith

Persons exercising authority under the Company Agreement shall act in good faith and in accordance with the covenant of good faith and fair dealing established in the Operating Agreement.

### 6.2 Scope of Authority

Persons exercising authority shall act only within the scope of their authority as established by the Operating Agreement, this Charter, or Resolution.

Actions outside the scope of authority do not bind the Company.

### 6.3 No Self-Dealing; Recusal

Persons exercising authority shall not engage in self-dealing or transact for personal benefit.

Where a person exercising authority has a conflict of interest or would be subject to legal constraint in relation to a matter, that person shall recuse themselves from participation in that matter.

### 6.4 Safe Harbor

Persons exercising authority are not liable for losses arising from good faith actions within the scope of their authority, except in cases of Fraud or Willful Misconduct.

### 6.5 Mandatory Law

No person is required to take any action that would cause that person to breach mandatory law. A good faith refusal to act on this basis is not a breach of the Company Agreement.

---

## Article VII -- Force Majeure and Limitations

### 7.1 Force Majeure

The Company and persons exercising authority are not liable for any failure to perform or delay in performance caused by events beyond reasonable control, including:

1. natural disasters;
2. power, telecommunications, or internet outages;
3. outages, congestion, or defects affecting underlying blockchain networks or infrastructure;
4. failure, insolvency, or misconduct of third-party service providers not under direct control;
5. actions by state actors, including sanctions, asset freezes, or compulsory orders; and
6. compromise or failure of cryptographic primitives or security assumptions.

### 7.2 Mitigation

Persons exercising authority shall use commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate and respond to force majeure events, but are not in breach merely because such events occur.

### 7.3 Protective Suspension

Where a person exercising authority reasonably believes a force majeure event has occurred and that proceeding would endanger Company Property, that person may suspend or delay action while notifying the Company, without this constituting a breach.

### 7.4 No Audit-Based Warranty

The fact that the Company has commissioned or published security audits or reviews of Protocol IP does not create any warranty or duty beyond those expressly set out in the Company Agreement.

## Article VIII -- Standard Operating Procedures

### 8.1 SOP Registry

The Company may maintain standard operating procedures and similar operational artefacts in the SOP Registry to support safe and efficient treasury and governance operations.

### 8.2 Interim Adoption

SOPs may be adopted or amended on an interim basis by the Head Steward or Subcommittees within their existing authority. Interim adoption does not amend this Charter, the Operating Agreement, or any on-chain constraints, and must not be used to expand authority.

### 8.3 Periodic Ratification

The Company should periodically ratify interim SOP changes through an Operational Pack proposal. Each Operational Pack should include a scope statement, change log, content identifiers for each SOP, effective date, and review expectation.

### 8.4 Hierarchy

SOPs are Tertiary Instruments. In the event of conflict with Primary or Secondary Instruments, the higher-precedence instrument prevails.

## Article IX -- Performance Vehicle

### 9.1 Acknowledgment

The Company acknowledges that a portion of Membership Interests are subject to on-chain lockup (the **"Performance Vehicle"**). The Performance Vehicle is visible on-chain.

### 9.2 No Individual Ownership or Control

The Performance Vehicle is not owned or controlled by any individual.

### 9.3 Not Company Property

The Performance Vehicle is not Company Property and is not part of Company Treasury Accounts. The Company does not control the on-chain programs implementing the Performance Vehicle.

### 9.4 Protection

The protections established in Section 10.3 of the Operating Agreement apply to the Performance Vehicle. Any Resolution that would:

1. exclude Membership Interests subject to lockup from fully-diluted treatment;
2. selectively dilute or discriminate against such Membership Interests;
3. seize, redirect, claw back, encumber, or extinguish the Performance Vehicle;
4. assert ownership or control over the Performance Vehicle; or
5. direct any person to take action against the Performance Vehicle;

is beyond the scope of the Company's authority and is void.

---

## Article X -- Natural Person Protections

### 10.1 No Personal Liability for Development

No natural person who has contributed to the design, development, or deployment of Protocol IP shall have any personal liability for any debt, obligation, loss, or other liability of the Company solely by virtue of:

1. authoring or committing code;
2. proposing or drafting governance proposals, memoranda, or documentation; or
3. acting as a developer or technical advisor;

unless such person has expressly assumed such liability in a separate written agreement signed in their personal capacity.

### 10.2 No Personal Liability for Historical Coordination

No natural person shall have any personal liability for Protocol losses, bugs, or failures solely by virtue of:

1. coordinating or overseeing development;
2. selecting or instructing third-party developers or auditors; or
3. approving or signing deployment transactions;

except in cases of Fraud or Willful Misconduct established by final judgment.

### 10.3 Fraud and Willful Misconduct

The protections in this Article do not apply to Fraud or Willful Misconduct.

## Article XI -- Amendment and Hierarchy

### 11.1 Company Agreement

This Charter is part of the Company Agreement. The Company Agreement comprises:

- **Primary Instruments:** the Operating Agreement, this Founding Charter, and the IP License Framework;
- **Secondary Instruments:** the Smart Contract Registry, Website Registry, Intellectual Property Registry, IP License & Assignment Register, and the Counterparty Agreements Register; and
- **Tertiary Instruments:** the SOP: Order of Business for Operational Packs, and the SOP Registry.

Proposal Memoranda serve as filing wrappers for amendments and other governance actions; their explanatory content is not normative but is subject to the hierarchy below to the extent of any conflict.

### 11.2 Hierarchy

In the event of conflict between instruments of the Company Agreement, the order of precedence is:

1. mandatory law;
2. Primary Instruments (in order: Operating Agreement, Founding Charter, IP License Framework);
3. Secondary Instruments; and
4. Tertiary Instruments; and
5. Proposal Memoranda.

### 11.3 Amendment

This Charter may be amended by Resolution in accordance with Section 6.5 of the Operating Agreement.

### 11.4 Interpretation

This Charter must be interpreted as a whole with the other instruments of the Company Agreement.

### 11.5 Protocol IP

The Company Agreement and all instruments incorporated by reference constitute Protocol IP. Unauthorized use, reproduction, or adaptation is an infringement of the Company's intellectual property rights and may be enforced by the Company.
